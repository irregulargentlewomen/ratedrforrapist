Roman Polanski (1933- ): French film director and screenwriter. His second wife, actress Sharon Tate (1943-69), was one of the victims of a multiple murder by followers of cult leader Charles Manson. Has lived in Europe since fleeing the United States in 1978 after pleading guilty to a plea bargain charge of "unlawful sexual conduct with a minor" (i.e., drugging and raping a thirteen-year-old girl). Notable movies: Rosemary's Baby (1968), Chinatown (1974), Tess (1979), and The Pianist (Academy Award, 2002).
